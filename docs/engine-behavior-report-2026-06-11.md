## 2. The four findings

### A. The suggestion strip DROPS the correct word (biggest single bug)
On append-o keys (`bangladesho`, `oneko`, `prodhano`) the editor commits the right word, but the
10-slot strip filters it out: **81% of primary-correct append-o keys lose the correct word from
the strip entirely** (1,651 of 2,028). Verified live: `bangladesho` commits বাংলাদেশ but the strip
shows বাংলাদেশকে/বাংলাদেশে/বাংলাদেশো — everything except বাংলাদেশ; `prodhano`'s strip puts the
misspelling প্রধাণ at #1 while filtering প্রধান out. Cause: the cleanliness/phonetic-fit filters +
re-sort (SmartEngine.kt:1319-1327) treat the o-mismatch as suspicious. **This is an engine fix,
not a data fix, and accounts for most of H7's 58.9% weighted miss.**

### B. The missing habit aliases are compiler-side, with zero ranking involvement
For every lazy class, the loss splits as: key absent from index, or key owned by a different real
word — **0% "present but outranked."** The general fix lives in ONE place
(`PhoneticIndexBuilder.aliasesFor`), not in the engine:
- H5: `sh→s` aliases (and chh→ch) — but `suru`/`sekh` are REAL word collisions, so habit aliases
  must compete on frequency, not replace
- H6: geminate-reduction aliases (`utor`→উত্তর, `chotogram`→চট্টগ্রাম)
- H7: trailing-o add/drop aliases both directions
- H8: j-aliases for জ-words (z-keys currently resolve only to য-words)

### C. Ten invented strings still leak past the gate
All 10 from two patterns: trailing-য় duplication from suffix recovery (তৃতীয়য়, আওতায়্য়) and
z→য composites (যাতির, যেলায়). Layer-6/root-composition outputs carrying `DICTIONARY` source
that the gate trusts. Same class as F2 — one more escape hatch to close.

### D. The dictionary itself has ~12.6k Unicode-duplicate rows
Composed vs decomposed nukta forms of the same word (484,996 → 472,348 after normalization).
Web-pasted text is decomposed; the dictionary is mixed. Compile-time normalization would
deduplicate and make all comparisons canonical.

## 3. Proposed general solution (for discussion)

**Tier the keys, don't multiply them blindly.** Add a `priority` column to the phonetic index:
- priority 0 = canonical keys (what the word's spelling actually romanizes to)
- priority 1 = habit-alias keys (the H5/H6/H7/H8 transforms, generated by ONE rule table)

Lookup ranks by (priority, frequency): a habit alias never hijacks a word whose canonical key
is what you typed (`suru` stays সুরু first), but fills the gap when nothing canonical matches
(`sastho` finds স্বাস্থ্য). The strip shows both tiers. This is the data-driven generalization of
what accha/assa always needed — habits become a TABLE, not code.

Plus three engine fixes: (1) strip filter must never drop a primary-correct word (Finding A);
(2) close the trailing-য় suffix-recovery leak (Finding C); (3) compile-time nukta dedupe (D).

**Projected impact** (from the key-absent split): H5/H6/H9 → ~90%+ primary; H7 → 95%+ top-3;
overall lazy-typist experience approaches the canonical 97.9%.

## 4. Suggestion-strip depth (UX datum)
When the correct word is at rank 2-10, it's at rank 2 in 58% and within rank 3 in 76% of cases.
Top-3 is the metric that matters; rank 4+ is nearly invisible to users.
